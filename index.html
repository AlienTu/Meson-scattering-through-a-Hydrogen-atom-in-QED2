Meson scattering through a QED2 hydrogen atom

Classical real-time experiment for the bosonized two-flavour massive Schwinger model. The page relaxes a one-atom static background near the origin, then injects a meson signal from the left and records outgoing fields near \(x=\pm40\).

The dynamic boundary now evolves only the perturbation \(\delta\phi=\phi-\phi_{\rm static}\) with an outgoing Sommerfeld condition. This is closer to an open 1+1D universe than a reflecting wall.

Model

\[\partial_t^2\phi_i-\partial_x^2\phi_i+\frac{g^2}{2}(\phi_1+\phi_2)+\sqrt{\pi}\,\kappa_i\sin(2\sqrt{\pi}\phi_i)=J_i(t,x),\quad i=1,2.\] \[x\in[-L/2,L/2],\qquad \Delta x=0.1\text{ by default}.\]
